Cinnamon Buns: Simple and Delicious

What are cinnamon buns?

Cinnamon buns are a simple and delicious pastry, that can be eaten at anytime of the day! It can be as a delicious breakfast, snack, or dessert.

Most cinnamon buns consist of a soft and fluffy dough rolled in cinnamon brown sugar, creating decadent layers of dough and sugar. The buns can have raisins mixed into the brown sugar to compliment the cinnamon. After the cinnamon buns are baked, they may be topped with a sweet cream cheese icing.

Cinnamon buns are not limited to the simple cinnamon bun, there are a wide variety of creative and delicious options. We have recipes for eggnog cinnamon buns, caramel topped cinnamon buns, etc. Whatever you can think of we may have it or can include it in our site.

Time to spice up your baking skills!

Cinnamon buns are a good starter pastry for those that are wanting to learn more about baking with yeast. They are also great for experienced bakers who want to be creative. We have found a variety of recipes to cover any skillset and interest.

Whether you are looking for something easy, homemade, gourmet, or something for the holidays, we have the recipe for you. Please check out the following links to discover something new.

How to improve store-bought refrigerated cinnamon buns

Lately I have been craving cinnamon buns, but I haven’t had time to make them from scratch, so I have been looking at ways of improving the taste of store bought refrigerated cinnamon buns. I was ecstatic when I discovered this article 4 Easy Ways to Make Refrigerated Cinnamon Rolls Taste Homemade by C. Thi Nguyen from the Greatist.

The article lists four great ways of improving store bought cinnamon buns such as what toppings to use, added inside extras, being inventive with frosting, and changing its shape. You can do things such as using a maple buttercream frosting and adding pecans inside or make a cinnamon bun with eggnog frosting for the perfect holiday treat. The possibilities are endless.

The following quote from the article describes a few simple changes that can potentially improve refrigerated cinnamon buns: “Cinnamon buns don’t have to stay in their original shape, either: you can turn them into one giant cinnamon bun for a fun, shareable breakfast centerpiece; make them in a waffle iron for crisp outsides before drizzling with icing or maple syrup; or roll small chunks of cinnamon roll dough into monkey bread”.

I am always encouraging people to experiment with their baking because that is how some of the best discoveries are made. Without experimenting we wouldn’t have the recipes we know and love. I wouldn’t be able to post about eggnog cinnamon buns or caramel frosted cinnamon buns, both of which are absolutely delicious. I honestly wouldn’t have thought of half the baking recipes that I come across but because of someone else’s innovation and experimentation, I have a tonne of recipes that I absolutely love and hope that you feel the same way.

It always astonishes me when people add things that theoretically should never work with your baked good but ends up being absolutely delicious, such as bacon on cinnamon buns and doughnuts.
